# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students

As a student navigating a highly digital world, it is crucial to proactively protect your personal data, devices, and finances. Follow this actionable checklist to stay secure.

## General Online Safety
* **Use strong, unique passwords & enable 2FA:** Never reuse passwords across your university portal, social media, and banking apps. Always enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) wherever possible.
* **Verify before you click:** Scammers often spoof official college email addresses. Always double-check the actual sender address, not just the display name, especially for "urgent" messages or unsolicited job offers.
* **Avoid sensitive tasks on public Wi-Fi:** Do not log into your bank or pay college fees while connected to free, unsecured Wi-Fi at campus cafes or hostels. Use your mobile data instead.
* **Limit personal oversharing:** Be cautious about sharing your exact location, phone number, or daily schedule on social media, as scammers use these details for social engineering.
* **Update your devices:** Always keep your smartphone, laptop OS, and apps updated. These updates often contain critical patches for new security vulnerabilities.
* **Download from trusted sources only:** Avoid pirating software or downloading textbooks from shady websites, as they often contain hidden malware or ransomware.

## Financial & UPI Safety 
* **Never enter your UPI PIN to *receive* money:** This is the golden rule of UPI. You only need to enter your PIN when you are sending money or checking your balance. 
* **Do not scan random QR codes for cashbacks:** Scanning a QR code on PhonePe, GPay, or Paytm is solely for *paying* someone. Legitimate cashbacks will never require you to scan a code or enter your PIN.

## Emergency Reporting Channels
If you or a peer fall victim to a cybercrime or financial fraud, act immediately:
* **Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline:** Dial **1930** immediately to report financial fraud and potentially freeze the stolen funds.
* **Register a formal complaint:** Visit the **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal** at [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) to file a detailed report.
