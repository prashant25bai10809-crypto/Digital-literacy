# Case Study: The "Student Job" Phishing Scam

**What the crime is:**
Employment phishing is a targeted cybercrime where attackers impersonate legitimate employers or university staff to deceive students into handing over personal information or money. Scammers exploit the financial anxieties of college students by offering fake, "too good to be true" remote jobs with high pay for minimal effort.

**How it typically happens:**
1. **The Hook:** A student receives an unsolicited email sent directly to their university address. The sender often spoofs an official campus domain or claims to have found the student through a "university directory."
2. **The Lure:** The email offers an attractive part-time job (e.g., $400/week for remote administrative work) and immediately "hires" the student without an interview. 
3. **The Catch:** The scammer mails a counterfeit check to the student with instructions to deposit it into their personal bank account. 
4. **The Theft:** The scammer then directs the student to use a portion of those funds to buy "office supplies" from a specific vendor or send money to a third party via untraceable gift cards or wire transfers.

**Who is usually targeted:**
College students are prime targets because they frequently seek flexible, part-time work to help cover tuition and living expenses. Furthermore, they are more likely to trust communications that land in their official academic inboxes.

**The consequences:**
Banks often make deposited funds available within a day, but it takes weeks to verify a check. Once the counterfeit check inevitably bounces, the bank reverses the deposit. The student is then held entirely responsible for the funds they already transferred to the scammer, often losing hundreds or thousands of dollars of their own money and facing severe credit damage or identity theft.
